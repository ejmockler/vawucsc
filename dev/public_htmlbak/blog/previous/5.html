﻿February 9, 2017 - Current Events Discussion: Peru's Infrastructure, Health Care, and Education

2/13/2017

0 Comments

 
For this week's discussion, we read "Infrastructure, Health Care, and Education: Key Priorities to Maintain Peru's Progress" from The Financial. 

Members were asked the following questions: 
  1. What is something you learned from this article? What is surprising to you? 
  2. Why do you think it is important for Peru to reconstruct their infrastructure? What aspects need to be improved first? What should be Peru's priority?

After members discussed in pairs, some people responded to the first question by just saying that they didn't know that Peru was in the progress of building its infrastructure. We were in disbelief that the article was recent as well. Our president then asked, "Why is that important to fix?". To which someone responded that Peru was compared to successful cities in the article and if they weren't prioritizing as aggressively as the other cities. People then started to suggest things that should happen. One comment was that the wealth distribution must be fixed by making more investments to the poorer communities. As VAW members, we are advocates for making communities safer and healthier, and we stand with the underserved communities in Peru. 

This leads into the next question. Before the health care and education can be changed, the infrastructure must be built. One member, Irvin, said that the infrastructure is the base of what can be built for Peru so that must be the priority. 

That's it for this discussion section!
