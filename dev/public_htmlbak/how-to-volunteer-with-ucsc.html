How can you volunteer with VAW at UCSC? 

VAW at UCSC can travel to any of the 7 locations listed on the Volunteer page. As a team, we will have to opportunity to choose the location we would like to travel to and the dates we would like to travel on. 

Each trip is limited to 25 members only. 
  • Members will be selected through an application process. 

Requirements and Club Activities

We do not require that you have previous experience in healthcare; however, we do highly encourage members to get their CPR, AED, BLS, and First Aid Certifications.
  • VAW at UCSC is currently looking into how members can receive this training together as a club.

The VAW club at UCSC organizes training sessions (how to do intakes, take blood pressure, measure weigh and height, language lessons). Members participating on the medical outreach trip are recommended to participate in all or most of these activities. 
  • While in your host country, you will receive separate training and skill lessons in preparation of clinic work. 

VAW-UCSC is also looking forward to working with other clubs on campus to increase the presence and awareness of our club. We will be inviting other clubs to come join us at our events and have some present their own club experiences and goals! 
​
On top of training sessions, we would like all members to consistently attend the general club meetings held every other week to stay updated with our trip plans, projects, volunteer opportunities and also all club activities throughout the year. Click here for more details. 

Funding Your Trip 

Fundraising will be a large part of our club activities throughout the year. It is each member's responsibility to make or pay their trip costs and fees: your share of the fundraising money is from your participation in the fundraising events. Each member will also have their own Empowered accounts where donations and payments can be made. 
 
​There will be sign up sheets for every fundraiser.  For this, members are recommended to stay updated with the upcoming events. 

​Break Down of Activities and Days In-country  

15 days/14 nights total for your trip
•2-3 Travel Days to get from the USA to your host
community
•6 Medical Work Days to work in mobile clinics
and learn about the local health care system
•1 Education Day to visit a local school or
community and teach a public health topic
•1 Orientation Day to prepare for your clinics
and practice your skills
•3-5 Free Days to explore your host country
