Purpose of Organization at University of California, Santa Cruz
Volunteers Around The World (VAW) is a social enterprise and national organization devoted to improving the standard of living in poverty-stricken communities around the world through means of medical outreach. Our focus is to provide communities with access to medical treatment, medication, nutritional security, and clean water. VAW runs mobile clinics in underserved communities around the world, creating humanitarian initiatives that provides free primary health care and health education. This chapter at UCSC will continue to expand these projects, recruiting volunteers that are interested in visiting different countries to learn about new cultures, shadowing doctors, exploring the health field, and to teach public health classes to children in vulnerable communities.
​
​VAW at UCSC emphasizes that members who go on this trip will have an exposure to new cultures and experiences as well as help those in vulnerable parts of the world. As club members (who may or may not necessarily go on this trip), it is important that we continue to spread awareness about these countries do not have the medical necessities that we are capable of providing and raising money for. This does not however in anyway devalue the imperative for medical assistance in communities around the United States as we recognize that disparities in the healthcare system are ubiquitous. In addition to medical outreach abroad, VAW at UCSC extends its efforts into the local health care system here in Santa Cruz, reaching out to local hospitals and free clinics for learning and working experiences. The goal is then to create both a local and global perspective that allows for a more comprehensive look into the health cares system in different communities around the world. We highlight in our club purpose that we as a community and club strive to better our understanding of cultures and a knowledge needed in order to make a change.
